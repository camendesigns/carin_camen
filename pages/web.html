Web

Exploring the Process


Web design and development consists of visual and interactive processes. The combination of the two provides a visually pleasing and informative experience.

My process begins with understanding the clients needs. This allows research to begin and be productive, gathering data about their customers, competition and marketing. Once this has been achieved I go into building the framework which provides structure to the process. A good design is achieved once the foundation has been laid. Depending if I will be developing the site, determines if I will produce a design doc. This document is critical to a programmer developing a site that has been designed by another person. Finally, a style guide is produced for the client to ensure that there is cohesion in the brand.

Site Map

Defining the Page Structure


The site map establishes the page navigation and how many pages will be designed and developed. Defining the page structure will streamline this process.

The development of a site map involves critical thinking of the layout and functions of the website. When discussions takes place in this initial phase, it assist the designer in knowing how each page should be design to provide cohesion to the site. The developer is assisted by obtaining knowledge of how the pages function and interact with each other.

Wireframe

Preliminary Design


The wireframe is the first visual mockup for the website. This design consists of blocks of color, each representing an area of the design.

In the wire-framing process, the discussion begins to flow towards the specifics of design. The incorporation of color, font and visuals will come later. This phase is done in black and white with blocks and lines representing areas and text. It can be produced in fine detail, or sketchout out.

Ps Mock-up

Defining the Page Structure


With the site-map and wireframe in hand, the next step of the process is to produce a Photoshop mockup of the site.

In the mock-up phase, color, font and visuals are integrated into the wireframe design. This phase will produce a static layout of the visual look of the website. Starting with the home page, usually three mock-ups are produced to provide the client with a visual choice. This dialogue in this discussion fine tunes the design of the site. Attention to the fine details of color, font and visual elements is flushed out in this phase. Once the final mock-up has been approved by the client, coding begins.

Design Doc

Defining the Page Structure


The Design Doc provides coding instructions for the developer of the site. The Ps Mockup has been cut up. Dimensions and spacing of the website is shown to assist in expediting the coding process.

In many cases, when the designer is also developing, this step can be optional. Some designer's and and developers choose to fine-tune their individuals skills. If design and development are separated, the creation of the design doc is critical. The thorough design document, will enable the designer to move onto the next project, allowing the developer to complete the website.

Style Guide

Defining the Page Structure


The Style Guide is a document to assist the client in maintaining the brand that has been created. In many instances, the development of a logo, color scheme, font choice and identity message has been produced.

This document contains the standards, used in developing the website. These standards may have been brought in by the client or produced for the client. To ensure that the company has a cohesive look throughout all their online and print campaigns, the implementation of standards found in the style guide must be adhered to.

The Final Phase


The final phase of the website process, includes tying all the online marketing together as well as the implementing SEO (Search Engine Optimization) so your customers can find you.

The purpose of SEO is to help search engines find your site. When doing an online search, keywords are attached to your site to assist in the heirachy of where your site is placed in the results. SEO can be done organically, where the results were obtain without paying. A good website design will incorporate organic SEO. Non-Organic SEO, artifically boost your results by paying for keywords and phrases.

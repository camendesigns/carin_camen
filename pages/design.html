Design

It all Begins with Design


Wherever we look, design is what provides the visual interest. From the bold and dramatic, to the soft and subdued, design creates an emotion that draws our attention.

Carin Camen Portfolio, is the fine arts arm of Camen Designs. In this setting I fully explore the fine arts to fine tune my skills that can be incorporated into my client work. Symbolism and metaphores are intertwined in my designs, bringing an emotional dimension to my work.

The Art of Design

7 Elements of Design


Elements of Design are used when creating art. They are the foundation that allows an artist to beautify their art piece. These elements can be used in combination, or by themselves. Their use surrounds us and can be found in nature, architecture and products we use on a daily basis.

Space is the area around, between or within It is either positive (white or light), or negative (black or dark. The use of space creates the illustion of dimension and depth by placing objects in layers of different sizes. White space provides an are around an objectâ€™s space to give the eye rest. Dark space creates depth.

Line is fundamental in design to define edges, divide space and decorate. it is used to show contour, gestures, expressions and to separate objects in an art piece. Line can be straight, curved, thick or thin. Line also provides shading to create depth and shadow through small short strokes call hatch strokes.

Color is a key element in design that creates a certain look and feel to the art piece. Color is broken down into hue, the spectrum of a color wheel. Intensity refers to the brightness and strength of the hue. Value is the darkness or lightness of the hue.

Value is the lightness or darkness of color. Regardless of whether an art piece is done in black and white or color will contain value. However, value is critical in black and white for it to succeed. The varying shades of black, grey and white allows the eye to see dimension and form.

Form relates to objects that are three-dimensional and has length, width and height. Form is different from shape. Shape is two dimensional. Form also provides cohesion of the elements in how the elements unite.

Shape is two dimensional and flat. It contains only height and width. Shapes can be organic with curves and free form. Organic shapes will have less-defined outlines. Shapes can also be geometric with precise and defined outlines. Tools can be used to create geometric shapes, whereas organic shapes are usually created by free hand.

Texture provides visual interest and depth. Texture can either be natural, where it can be felt on a three dimensional object, or implied to provide the look of texture on a two dimensional or flat surface. Texture can be created to provide a visual illusion of what an object would feel like. The use of illusional texture provides depth on a flat surface creating visual stimulation and appeal.

The Structure of Design

8 Principles of Design


The Principles of Design are the structural elements used in art to organize the design. These principles are applied to objects within the design. The success or failure of a design can be tied to the artistâ€™s ability in understanding how to apply the principles of design into their artwork.

Emphasis is a focal element of a design. Emphasis directs hierarchy. When emphasis is used, the eye is directed to the predominate part of the design and then is directed to the supporting elements of the design. Emphasis can be used in correlation with contrast, color and line.

Rhythm is used in harmony with pattern. It is the repetition of line, shape and color that provides unity to the design. Rhythm also helps to organize the design and can be accomplished in regular or irregular rhythm. Regular rhythm uses repetition in an even or slightly even pattern. Irregular rhythm uses repetition in an uneven manner. Both provide visual interest.

Pattern is the use of repetition to provide visual stimulation and appeal. Pattern is accomplished using line, shape and color in repetition. Pattern is achieved in various formats that harmonize with emphais and movement.

Contrast provides visual interest and excitement in art. It refers to varying difference in color, value, shape, size and texture. The use of light and dark, black and white creates the strongest contrast elements in art. Having contrasting and dissimilar objects in a design stimulates the eye and assists in creating dimension.

Balance relates to objects and how they are arranged. Balance can be symmetrical, with both sides equal and similar. It can also be asymmetrical where both sides are different from each other. Balance can also be used radially where the use of line and shapes draws the eye to a focal point.

Unity allows the art of design to have a sense of completeness. All the elements of the design work together in harmony. Unity combined with variety creates visual visual appeal. Together, they are the finishing touches of design.

Variety draws the eye into the design to obtain more information. When there is variety in the design, there is visual interest. If there is no variety of color, tones, hues or objects the design will look flat.

Movement allows the viewer to journey through the design. It can show action and motion. Using value, the eye can be directed towards a smaller part of the design that would normally go unnoticed. Movement is a supporting element of emphasis.

Broken


Working in a monochromatic color palette, the value is an essential element. The use of line, texture and contrast are amongst the elements and principles used in this design.

This design was achieved through layering. I began with creating a background texture. Next I placed my photography photo of a silver mannequin. On top of the mannequin I played with several cracked layers. Using some photoshop techniques, I masked out part of the upper layers to make the mannequin appear as if she is breaking through what has tried to hold her back. The final touch was the tear of joy that gently falls from her cheek, she has overcome.

Past the Storm


Working with color, automatically provides visual interest. Incorporating pattern, balance, variety and space helps give this design dimension.

This design was achieved by creating the sky background using a cloud brush. Upon this layer, the grass and flowers were created. Next came the various cloud layers, causing the sun to peak out behind the white clouds to show that the sun always shines after the storm. The final touch was to create the butterfly, to indicate a renewal that occurs when we never fear facing the storms of live.

The Edge


Creating an "edgy" design is speculative of personal artistic choice. Line, texture and value is heavily used in this piece.

This piece was created by developing the foundation layer of texture. I incorporated vector images made in Illustrator into Photoshop to provide detail and visual interest. The final step was go create another layer of texture to provide the effects on and around the skull. This design symbolically represents the embracing the failure as a learning experience that will help us to succeed on a greater level. Success comes through focus, hard work and learning from failures.
